# Snapshit::Goodbye



# Photos are the most important part of snapchat.

This is a reimagination of the GDPR required takeout feature that Snapchat offers on https://accounts.snapchat.com/v2/download-my-data .
We are going from a takeout kebab from behind the trainstation to a 5 course curated meal.

Snapchat's data export already gives the virtue of privacy in the form of knowing in which ways your privacy is currently comprimised by them. Atleast they are honest. Snapshit adds to the list the virtue of choice, ownership, and respect for your life memories.

It should not reflect the data structure of snapchats backend but reflect how we think about our memories. It should be chronological, it should respect a memory as a multimodal-primitive.Chat images should be mixed in with memories.
So that there should be a album based interface to view the photos and media from the archive.

Snapchat's export feature is great, it reflects the users right to own their own data and complies with privacy laws like GDPR. What its not designed to do is what people actually want to do with it. Which is to reflect, over the past, say goodbye in a meaningful way and save the real memories that actually mattered. I personally found great joy in coaming through the insights and appreciate that snapchat has taken the liberty of storing my life story on their severs free of charge for so many years.

The images of course is the most important thing, but without context a lot of the memes are worthless. So with each media element it should link to the chats, profiles etc.

There should also be filters to exclude photos from specific chats, not all memories are beautiful memories. The choice is yours, it is your life.

There should be a map of activity that you can look at. The bubbles should mean stuff.  You should be able to look at what happened in that time in Oslo. Whats your story there.
